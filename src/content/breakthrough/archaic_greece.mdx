The Greeks made attempts to describe limits. This concept appears in Euclid's work Elements.

Archimedes used similar ideas. For example, in his proof of the formula for the area of a circle. He considered inscribed and circumscribed polygons,
increasing the number of their sides to infinity, reducing the difference between the area of the polygons and the desired area of the circle.
However, it seems that the ancient Greeks did not fully grasp the concept of the limit.
